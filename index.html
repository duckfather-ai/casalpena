Cannes Lions

Ricardo Casal & Juan Javier Peña Plaza — a creative partnership across DAVID and GUT.

96
Cannes Lions. Three Grand Prix, across three disciplines, over nine years — built idea by idea.

The story of the 95

It started in Buenos Aires in 2016 with Manboobs — a breast-cancer self-exam that used men's chests to outwit social-media censorship, and won a Grand Prix for Good plus ten Golds in a single stroke.

A year later in Miami came Google Home of the Whopper, the 15-second ad that hijacked a million voice assistants and took the Direct Grand Prix. Those two ideas alone account for nearly a third of the haul.

Then the engine: Burger King, Heinz, Popeyes, NotCo, Michelob, DoorDash, Google — wave after wave of idea-led, earned-media work through the DAVID years and into GUT, from Miami to São Paulo to Mexico City. A pandemic pause in 2021, then the third Grand Prix: Real Tone, in Mobile, for making the camera see every skin tone.

Ninety-six Lions. Three Grand Prix. One partnership.

Conclusions & Learnings

What 96 Lions across nine years and two agencies actually reveal about how Casal Peña win — the patterns underneath the trophies.

28 Lions

Two ideas, nearly a third of everything

Manboobs and Google Home of the Whopper — both DAVID, both 2016–17 — account for ~30% of the entire haul. One won wide (≈15 different Lion categories at once), the other deep (10 Golds from a single idea). The record is built on outlier swings, not steady base hits.

~½

The 2016–17 supernova

Close to half of every Lion landed in just those two years. It's the clearest pattern in the data: when an idea is big enough it doesn't win one Lion — it sweeps a dozen across every discipline simultaneously. Scale of idea beats volume of output.

3 / 3

Three Grand Prix, three disciplines

Good (Manboobs, 2016), Direct (Google Home of the Whopper, 2017), Mobile (Real Tone, 2022). There's no "home" category — they don't win by owning a craft, they win wherever the idea naturally lives. Range, not specialism.

15

MACMA: the efficiency record

Fifteen Lions — including a Grand Prix — from just two charity ideas. Burger King is the volume engine of the body of work; MACMA is the efficiency one, the highest Lions-per-idea of the entire career.

0

Won on ideas, not craft

Zero craft Grand Prix. The metals cluster in Direct, Mobile, Social, PR, Media, Innovation and Health — the invention-and-earned-media end of the spectrum. Almost nothing in Film Craft, Print or Industry Craft. The award is for the thought, not the finish.

2

Two distinct winning modes

The purpose work breaks a taboo or a bias for a cause (Manboobs → breast-cancer detection; Real Tone → camera racial bias) and wins the Grand Prix. The brand work hijacks culture with an earned-media stunt (Google Home, BK BOT) and wins by volume. Same duo, two playbooks.

2021 → 0

A zero, then a rebuild

The pandemic took 2021 to nothing. What followed wasn't another supernova but a steady GUT-era cadence — Real Tone's Grand Prix plus a stream of 1–3-Lion ideas through 2025. Proof the engine survived the agency move intact.

→

DAVID built the peaks; GUT built the base

Every blockbuster and every Grand Prix is rooted in the DAVID years. GUT is prolific and consistent, but the next 14-Lion monster hasn't landed there yet — arguably the one chapter of this story still unwritten.

The throughline

Casal Peña don't win by being everywhere a little. They win by making a handful of ideas big enough to sweep an entire festival, and by knowing which ideas are cause — built to win a Grand Prix — and which are culture — built to win the room. Ninety-six Lions, three Grand Prix, one method: bet on the idea.

D&AD

Ricardo Casal & Juan Javier Peña Plaza — the Pencil record across DAVID and GUT.

37
D&AD Pencils. Across 16 ideas over a decade — topped by a Black Pencil, D&AD's rarest award.

The story of the 37

If Cannes rewards the splash, D&AD rewards the craft and the integrity of the idea — its Pencils are famously harder to win. And the record reaches the very top: Real Tone took a Black Pencil in 2022 — D&AD's rarest, most coveted award.

It opens just as strong: Manboobs won a White Pencil for social good and a Yellow — five Pencils across 2016–17. Then Google Home of the Whopper swept nine (three of them Yellow), and the Burger King × DAVID partnership itself won a Yellow for four years of work.

Thirty-seven Pencils. A Black. A White. Six Yellows. The summit, reached.

The One Show

Ricardo Casal & Juan Javier Peña Plaza — the Pencil & Merit record across DAVID and GUT.

46
One Show Pencils. Across 17 winners — crowned by a Penta Pencil and two Best of Discipline, the show's rarest honors.

The story of the 46

The One Show's Gold Pencil is one of advertising's most coveted prizes — and in 2018 Google Home of the Whopper didn't just win one, it won ten, plus a Best of Discipline — the single most-decorated idea anywhere in this entire record, at any festival.

That same year, the Burger King × DAVID partnership took the show's Penta Pencil — awarded to the body of work that scored the most points all year. It started with Manboobs4Boobs' two Golds and ran through the BK engine into the GUT era — Save It See It, Vanguards of Photography and Real Tone, which took a second Best of Discipline.

One Penta Pencil. Two Best of Discipline. Nineteen Golds. Forty-six Pencils.

What the One Show record reveals — where the partnership reached its single highest peak of decoration.

12

The most-decorated idea, anywhere

Google Home of the Whopper took a Best of Discipline, 10 Golds and a Silver — twelve One Show awards from one idea. No single idea won more at any festival in the entire record. The One Show loved it even harder than Cannes.

★★

Two Best of Discipline — one off the summit

One Show's Best of Discipline sits just below Best of Show — the top of a whole craft category. Google Home of the Whopper and Real Tone each took one — putting this work in the rarest company the show offers, twice over.

★

A Penta Pencil — the partnership prize

The One Show's Penta Pencil goes to the body of work that scores the most points across the whole year. In 2018 the Burger King × DAVID partnership won it outright with 75 points — a rare honor for the agency–client relationship itself.

19

Nineteen Golds, two eras

The Golds cluster in the DAVID years (Google Home, Manboobs) but GUT keeps scoring them — Vanguards of Photography, Save It See It and Real Tone all landed Gold. The gold-standard idea survived the agency move.

↗

The idea travels — three festivals

Real Tone is the proof: a Cannes Mobile Grand Prix, a D&AD Black Pencil, and a One Show Best of Discipline — the top honor at all three. Google Home of the Whopper did the same: Direct GP, nine D&AD Pencils, One Show Best of Discipline + 10 Golds. Strong ideas don't depend on one jury — or one festival.

BK

Burger King, the through-line engine

As at Cannes and D&AD, the bulk of the One Show haul traces to the Burger King partnership of the DAVID years — the relationship that minted the records across every show.

The throughline

The One Show is where a single idea reached its highest concentration of honors — Google Home of the Whopper's Best of Discipline + 10 Golds. Across three festivals the verdict is the same: a handful of ideas, big enough to sweep, judged great by every jury that saw them. 46 One Show Pencils — a Penta, two Best of Discipline, nineteen Golds — one more proof it's the idea that travels.

Three of advertising's most respected festivals — Cannes Lions, D&AD and The One Show — each with its own jury, its own crafts, its own currency of pencils and lions. Put side by side, the same ideas keep winning at all three.
